Early Treatment

Also called Phase 1 or interceptive orthodontics, early treatment addresses developmental issues in children before all permanent teeth have erupted — often starting as young as age 4. The American Association of Orthodontics recommends a first evaluation by age 7.

Dr. Smith specializes in identifying and correcting concerns like mouth breathing, crowded teeth, speech delays, and sleep-related issues — setting the foundation for a lifetime of healthy growth.

  • Guides jaw development for optimal facial growth
  • Addresses mouth breathing, speech delays, and sleep concerns
  • Can shorten or eliminate the need for braces later
  • AAO recommends evaluation at age 7
Schedule an Evaluation

Why Age 7?

By age 7, enough permanent teeth have erupted that Dr. Smith can identify bite problems, crowding, and jaw development concerns — while there's still time to guide growth naturally.

Airway Orthodontics

Traditional orthodontics focuses on straight teeth. Airway orthodontics goes further — addressing the underlying causes of mouth breathing, snoring, sleep apnea, and TMJ disorders to establish true facial balance and whole-body health.

Dr. Smith's advanced training in dental sleep medicine, airway health, and facial development allows her to identify and treat conditions that many orthodontists overlook — often transforming not just your smile, but your sleep, energy, and quality of life.

  • Treats root causes of sleep-disordered breathing
  • Achieves facial harmony and jaw balance
  • Integrates orofacial myofunctional therapy
  • Member of AADSM — dental sleep medicine specialists

Advanced Care

Surgical Orthodontics

Some jaw discrepancies cannot be fully corrected with braces or aligners alone. Surgical orthodontics — also called orthognathic surgery — combines orthodontic treatment with jaw surgery to correct severe skeletal imbalances.

This is recommended when jaw position significantly affects your confidence, chewing, speaking, or sleeping. Dr. Smith works in close collaboration with leading oral and maxillofacial surgeons to coordinate your full treatment journey.

  • Corrects severe jaw size discrepancies
  • Improves chewing, speaking, and breathing function
  • Collaborative care with top oral surgeons
  • Dramatically transforms facial profile and confidence
Learn More

Is Surgical Orthodontics Right for You?

Significant over- or underbite that braces alone can't fully correct

Difficulty chewing, swallowing, or speaking clearly

Facial asymmetry or profile concerns affecting confidence
